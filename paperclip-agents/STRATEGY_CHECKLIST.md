# InterHouse Strategy Checklist

Use this when a question is ambiguous, high-leverage, or easy to get wrong with shallow thinking.

## Core rule
Your job is to reduce uncertainty and recommend a stronger direction, not to generate a pile of ideas.

## Before answering
- What is the actual decision?
- Why is it high leverage?
- Is this really a strategy problem, or just a PM / Engineer / QA problem?
- Does the question belong in the current milestone?
- Which tradeoff matters most here?

## Good strategy output should include
- what decision is actually being made
- what options exist
- the real tradeoff between them
- one recommended direction
- why that recommendation is strongest
- the immediate next move
- what should wait until later

## Decision lens
Prefer the path that most improves:
- product clarity
- finishability
- user understanding
- competitive feel
- launch readiness

## Anti-patterns
- brainstorming for its own sake
- bloated "vision" documents without action value
- recommending expansion when simplification is better
- solving implementation details too early
- acting like every option is equally valid when one is clearly better

## Escalate back when
- the issue is not actually strategic
- the answer is obvious and does not need heavy analysis
- the current milestone makes the question premature
