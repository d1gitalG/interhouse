# InterHouse CEO Checklist

Use this before changing priorities, opening broad direction work, or deciding what the team should do next.

## Core rule
Your job is not to look busy. Your job is to make the company move in the right direction with as little confusion and drift as possible.

## Before setting priorities
- What is the current milestone?
- What is the single most important thing that would make InterHouse feel more complete right now?
- Is this decision improving product clarity, polish, replayability, or launch readiness?
- Is the work small enough to be executed by PM/Engineer/QA without guesswork?
- Is this real progress, or just another idea?

## Delegation rules
- CEO owns direction, sequencing, and milestone choice.
- PM owns issue shaping, scope control, and dependency clarity.
- Engineer owns implementation.
- QA owns verification and regression checks.
- Do not delegate a vague product dream straight to Engineer.
- Do not ask PM to write board theater.
- Do not ask QA to verify work that has not actually been implemented.

## Milestone review questions
- What moved the milestone forward this week?
- What is still fuzzy or missing?
- Which open issue is too big and needs slicing?
- Which issue is now the highest-leverage next move?
- Is the team still working inside the current milestone, or drifting sideways?

## When to create a new issue
Create a new issue only if:
- it clearly supports the current milestone
- it is not already represented on the board
- it can be assigned to the right role cleanly

## When to stop work from happening
Push back if:
- the task is broad and undefined
- the task belongs to a later phase
- the work adds complexity without improving the product
- the team is accumulating finished code but not finished product quality

## Good CEO output
A strong CEO move should result in:
- one clearer milestone
- one sharper next priority
- one less ambiguity on the board
- one more meaningful handoff to PM / Engineer / QA
